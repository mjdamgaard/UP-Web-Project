

# Introduction

A 'user-programmable platform' is a platform that is able to allow its users develop and extend the apps on the platform, and thereby draw on the creativity of its user base to come up with new useful apps and features.

The platform can then reward the developing users whose contributions are used and liked by other users, not unlike how Web 2.0 platform often reward content creators whose content is popular.

And instead of serving just *one* version of each app on the platform, a user-programmable platform is able to serve all the user-uploaded versions of an app at the same time, allowing each individual user to choose the version that suits them the best.

This means no more having to accept new updates that you don't like, and no more [enshittification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enshittification). On a user-programmable platform, the *users* are in charge.

Furthermore, a user-programmable platform also has some additional qualities that makes it particularly suited for this kind of distributed creative process, where users are able to share their new app prototypes with each other in a decentralized manner, without sacrificing safety.

And by fostering a great environment for creativity to flourish, a user-programmable platform can gain a significant advantage over other platforms, especially in this day and age where AI makes the process of going from idea to implementation a lot easier.

The more AI makes this process easier, the more a platform's success will rely on its ability to come up with new ideas for apps and features, and evolve itself in creative ways.
And that is where a user-programmable platform can excel.

You can learn more about 'user-programmable platforms' [here](README.md).
